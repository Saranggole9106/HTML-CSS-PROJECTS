Appearance around the world

White tigers, though native to India, are now found in captivity across the globe due to extensive breeding programs. Following the capture of the first known white tiger, Mohan, in Rewa, Madhya Pradesh, in 1951, zoos and private breeders began reproducing white tigers for public exhibition and conservation display. Today, white tigers are present in zoological parks, wildlife sanctuaries, and safari parks in countries such as the United States, China, Germany, Australia, and the United Arab Emirates. Some of the most famous white tigers have been featured in entertainment shows in Las Vegas, particularly by magicians like Siegfried & Roy, which increased global fascination with these animals. In China, white tigers are showcased as symbols of power and purity in zoos and breeding centers. Despite their global presence in captivity, white tigers are virtually nonexistent in the wild, primarily due to their rarity and lack of camouflage, which makes survival difficult. Their widespread appearance is therefore a result of human-led breeding, and not natural distribution, raising important ethical questions about the purpose of such practices in wildlife management and conservation.
